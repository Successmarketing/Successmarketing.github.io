Cake Boxes for Bakery Business: The Complete Guide

July 25, 2025 13 min read Boxes

In the bakery business, the cake box is not merely packaging — it is the first chapter of your customer's experience. A beautifully decorated cake arriving in a flimsy, ill-fitting box undermines hours of skilled work. Conversely, a well-chosen cake box enhances the perceived value of what is inside and builds the anticipation that makes unwrapping a cake a special moment.

India's bakery industry is booming. Home bakers have turned social media followings into thriving businesses, boutique bakeries are opening in every neighbourhood, and established bakery chains are expanding their delivery operations. All of them share a common need: reliable, attractive, and appropriately sized cake boxes. This guide covers everything you need to know.

Understanding Cake Box Anatomy

A good cake box is more than four walls and a lid. Each component plays a specific role in protecting and presenting the cake:

  • Base board: The sturdy bottom that supports the cake's weight. It must be rigid enough to prevent sagging, especially for heavy cakes with multiple layers or fondant decoration.
  • Walls: The sides of the box that protect the cake from lateral impact and temperature changes. Wall height should exceed the cake height by at least 2-3 cm to prevent the lid from touching the top of the cake.
  • Lid: The top panel that protects the cake and is often the most visible part of the packaging. Lids may be flat, windowed (with a transparent panel), or integrated (folding construction).
  • Cake board: A separate cardboard circle or square placed inside the box, on which the cake sits. This allows the cake to be lifted out cleanly and provides additional structural support.

Cake boxes in India follow standard sizing that corresponds to common cake sizes. Here is a comprehensive size chart:

The golden rule of cake box sizing: the box should be 1-2 inches wider and 2-3 inches taller than the cake. This provides clearance for decorations, frosting, and any toppers while allowing the cake to sit centred without touching the walls.

Materials for Cake Boxes

Duplex Board

Duplex board is the most widely used material for cake boxes in India. It consists of two layers of paper board — a white-coated outer surface for printing and a grey inner surface. Standard duplex board for cake boxes ranges from 300-400 GSM (grams per square meter), with heavier boards used for larger cakes that require more structural support.

Duplex board offers a good balance of strength, print quality, and cost. It can be easily coated, laminated, or embossed for premium finishes. Most neighbourhood bakeries and mid-range cake shops use duplex board boxes.

Corrugated Board

For heavy cakes (above 2 kg), tall cakes, and tiered cakes, corrugated board provides essential additional strength. The fluted inner layer absorbs impacts and distributes weight more evenly than solid board. Corrugated cake boxes are standard for delivery of large celebration cakes, wedding cakes, and custom-designed cakes.

The trade-off is a less refined surface for printing, though modern E-flute corrugated boxes offer much better print quality than older B-flute options.

Rigid Board (Gift Box Style)

Premium bakeries and luxury cake brands use rigid board boxes — thick, non-collapsible boxes that feel substantial and expensive. These are similar in construction to gift boxes, with a separate lid and base, often finished with fabric covering, magnetic closures, or ribbon ties.

Rigid boxes cost significantly more (Rs 50-200+ per box) but create an unboxing experience that justifies premium cake pricing. They are reusable, which adds perceived value — customers often repurpose them as storage boxes.

Transparent/PET Boxes

Clear plastic cake boxes made from PET or RPET are increasingly popular for small cakes, cupcakes, and pastries where visual appeal is the primary selling point. The customer can see exactly what they are getting, which works well for retail display and impulse purchases.

Transparent boxes are ideal for cupcake sets (4-pack, 6-pack, 12-pack configurations), individual pastries, cake slices, and small decorated cakes. They are less suitable for large, heavy cakes due to structural limitations.

Window Boxes: The Best of Both Worlds

Window cake boxes combine the structural strength of cardboard with the visual appeal of transparency. A clear acetate or PET window in the lid allows customers to view the cake without opening the box, while the cardboard construction provides proper protection and support.

Window boxes are particularly effective for:

  • Retail display at bakery counters
  • Home bakers selling through social media (customers can verify the cake matches the photo)
  • Gift cakes where the recipient should see the design without opening
  • Bakeries with self-service display areas

The window can be positioned on the top (most common), front, or both. A top window shows cake decorations and writing, while a front window shows the layers and height of the cake.

Cake Boards and Internal Supports

The internal components of your cake box setup are just as important as the box itself:

Cake Boards

A cake board is a flat, food-grade board placed inside the box on which the cake sits. Standard cake boards are 2-3 mm thick corrugated board covered with food-grade foil (silver or gold). The board should be the same size or slightly larger than the cake to provide a clean edge for presentation and a stable base for cutting.

Cake Drums

For heavier cakes (above 2 kg), cake drums offer more support than standard boards. Drums are typically 10-12 mm thick and provide a rigid, level platform that prevents the cake from sagging. They are essential for stacked cakes, fondant-covered cakes, and any design with heavy decorations.

Internal Spacers

For tall cakes or cakes with delicate top decorations, internal spacers — small cardboard supports placed between the cake and the lid — prevent lid contact. Some cake box designs include built-in spacers, but for standard boxes, a simple cardboard strip taped across the inside of the lid works effectively.

Packaging for Safe Cake Delivery

Cake delivery is one of the most challenging food delivery tasks. Cakes are fragile, temperature-sensitive, and often expensive — a damaged cake means a disappointed customer and a potentially costly replacement. Here are essential practices for safe cake delivery:

Securing the Cake in the Box

  • Place a small dab of frosting or royal icing between the cake board and the box base. This prevents the cake from sliding inside the box.
  • For extra-delicate cakes, place non-slip shelf liner between the cake board and box base.
  • Ensure the cake is centred in the box with equal clearance on all sides.

Sealing the Box

  • Use tape or stickers to seal the box closed. An unsealed box can pop open during transport, with disastrous results.
  • Apply tamper-evident seals across the opening edge so the customer knows the box has not been opened during delivery.
  • Do not over-seal — the customer should be able to open the box without needing scissors or excessive force.

Transport Considerations

  • Cake boxes should always travel flat on a level surface. Instruct delivery partners never to tilt or stack heavy items on top of cake boxes.
  • For two-wheeler delivery (common in India), a flat carrier or pizza-style delivery bag with a level base is essential.
  • In hot weather, consider using an insulated bag to prevent frosting from melting during transit.
  • For premium or tall cakes, hand-delivery (not platform delivery) is strongly recommended.

Custom Printing and Branding for Bakeries

Your cake box is a powerful branding tool, especially in the home baker and boutique bakery segment where word-of-mouth and social media drive most new business.

  • Logo placement: Centre-top of the lid is the prime position. This is what gets photographed when customers share their cake on social media.
  • Colour scheme: Match your box colours to your brand identity. Pastel colours and elegant typography work well for premium bakeries, while bold colours and playful designs suit casual bakery brands.
  • Handles: A built-in handle or a separate carry handle makes the box easier to transport and gives it a gift-like quality that customers appreciate.
  • Care instructions: Print storage and serving instructions on the box interior. "Store in refrigerator. Remove 30 minutes before serving for best taste." This small detail reduces customer inquiries and prevents food safety issues.
  • Social media handles: Include your Instagram handle prominently. When customers photograph the cake in its box, your handle is right there for their followers to see.

Common Cake Box Mistakes

  1. Box too tight: A box that barely fits the cake risks smearing frosting against the walls during transport. Always leave 1-2 inches of clearance.
  2. Box too large: An oversized box allows the cake to slide freely during transport. Use a correctly sized box, and secure the cake board to the box base.
  3. Lid too low: The lid touching decorations or a written message on top of the cake is one of the most common complaints. Measure the total height including toppers and decorations.
  4. Weak base for heavy cakes: A 3 kg fondant cake on a thin duplex board base will sag in the centre. Use corrugated boxes or cake drums for heavy cakes.
  5. No board under the cake: Placing a cake directly on the box bottom without a cake board makes it nearly impossible to remove cleanly. Always use a cake board.
