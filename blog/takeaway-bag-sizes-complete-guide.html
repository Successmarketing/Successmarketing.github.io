Takeaway Bag Sizes: Complete Guide for Food Businesses

August 22, 2025 13 min read How-To

The takeaway bag is the last thing your kitchen touches and the first thing your customer sees. It is simultaneously a practical carrier, a branding surface, and a signal of quality. Yet many food businesses treat bag selection as an afterthought, grabbing whatever is cheapest or whatever the supplier has in stock. The result is bags that tear under the weight of food containers, bags that are comically oversized for a single sandwich, or bags that stain through with grease before the customer reaches home.

This guide covers every standard takeaway bag size in the Indian market, explains the measurement system, and recommends the right size for different food orders and business types.

How Takeaway Bags Are Measured

Takeaway bags are measured by three dimensions: width (W), depth/gusset (D), and height (H). Width is the face of the bag, depth is how far the bag extends when opened (also called the gusset), and height is measured from the bottom to the top of the bag, excluding handles.

These dimensions are typically expressed in inches in the Indian market, though centimetres are used by some manufacturers. The standard format is W x D x H (e.g., 8 x 4 x 10 inches). Some suppliers list dimensions as W x H x D, so always confirm the order with your supplier to avoid confusion.

Flat Paper Bag Sizes

Flat paper bags (no gusset) are used for simple items that do not need much depth: sandwiches, wraps, burgers, bread, and dry snacks. They are the most economical bag option.

Kraft Paper Bags: The Premium Choice

Kraft bags have become the default choice for quality-conscious restaurants and cafes. Made from unbleached kraft paper, they offer a natural, premium look and are stronger than standard white or brown paper bags of the same weight. The brown kraft aesthetic signals eco-consciousness, which resonates with urban Indian consumers.

Kraft bags are available in the same size range as standard paper bags. The key difference is the paper weight (GSM). Standard kraft bags for food use are 80-120 GSM. For heavier loads (meals with multiple containers), 120-150 GSM bags are recommended.

Choosing the Right Bag Size for Your Business

Quick Service Restaurants (QSR)

QSR chains typically standardise on 2-3 bag sizes to cover their entire menu. A small bag (7 x 4 x 9) handles individual burgers and snack combos. A medium bag (8 x 5 x 10) covers standard meal deals. A large bag (10 x 6 x 12) handles family buckets and party orders. This three-size approach simplifies inventory and training while covering all order types.

Indian Restaurants and Dhabas

Indian food takeaway is characterised by multiple containers per order (rice, curry, dal, bread, sides). The medium to large bag range (8 x 5 x 12 to 12 x 7 x 16) handles most orders. A common mistake is using bags that are too small, forcing staff to cram containers in and risk them toppling over. It is better to size up slightly and have the containers sit comfortably.

Bakeries and Sweet Shops

Bakeries have diverse needs. Small flat bags work for individual pastries and cookies. Medium gusseted bags handle cake boxes and multiple pastry purchases. For cake boxes specifically, the bag needs to be sized so the box sits flat on the bottom without tilting. For mithai shops, see our sweet box packaging guide.

Cloud Kitchens

Most cloud kitchens rely on delivery platform riders who have their own bags, so a customer-facing bag is often unnecessary. However, some cloud kitchens use a simple kraft bag as an outer wrapper to keep containers together and add branding. A medium gusseted bag (8 x 5 x 12) covers most single-meal orders.

Non-Woven and Fabric Bags

While not disposable, non-woven polypropylene bags are increasingly used by restaurants for premium takeaway and repeat customers. They are reusable, washable, and serve as a long-term branding tool since customers reuse them for shopping and storage.

Grease Resistance and Food Safety

For any bag that will directly contact food (wraps, burgers placed directly in bags) or carry oily food containers, grease resistance is essential. Standard paper bags will stain through within minutes when exposed to oil, which looks unprofessional and weakens the bag.

Grease-resistant bags use either a food-grade PE coating on the inside surface or are made from naturally grease-resistant kraft paper. The coating adds Rs 0.30-0.50 per bag at wholesale quantities but prevents embarrassing grease stains on your customer's clothes and hands.

Printing and Branding

Takeaway bags are walking advertisements for your restaurant. A branded bag carried down a busy street is seen by hundreds of potential customers. Custom printing options range from a simple one-colour logo stamp (Rs 0.50-1.00 extra per bag) to full-colour printing (Rs 2-5 extra per bag). Even the most budget-conscious restaurants should consider at least a stamp with their name and phone number. For comprehensive branding strategies, see our food packaging branding guide.
