Ola Foods Packaging Requirements for Restaurant Partners in India

July 22, 2025 13 min read Industry

Ola, known primarily for its ride-hailing business, has expanded into food delivery and quick commerce, competing with Swiggy and Zomato in Indian cities. Ola Foods and its associated ventures represent a growing channel for restaurants and cloud kitchens looking to diversify beyond the two dominant platforms. For restaurant partners, understanding Ola's packaging expectations is essential to maintaining quality, compliance, and customer satisfaction on this platform.

This guide covers the packaging requirements specific to Ola's food delivery operations, the practical considerations that differ from other platforms, and how to set up your packaging for success on Ola.

Ola Foods: Platform Context

Ola's approach to food delivery is shaped by its background in logistics and transportation. The company understands last-mile delivery at scale, and it brings that operational mindset to its food business. For restaurant partners, this means that Ola tends to focus heavily on operational efficiency: fast pickup times, efficient delivery routes, and minimal order issues.

Ola leverages its existing driver network for food deliveries. This means the delivery partner picking up your food order is the same type of partner who drives Ola cabs and handles Ola's other logistics services. The implications for packaging are significant: these delivery partners are experienced in handling items during transit but may not have the food-specific training that dedicated food delivery partners receive.

Mandatory Packaging Standards on Ola Foods

FSSAI Compliance

Like all food delivery platforms operating in India, Ola requires its restaurant partners to hold valid FSSAI licenses. The license number must be visible on food packaging. Ola verifies FSSAI compliance during the restaurant onboarding process and can suspend listings for non-compliance. Display your FSSAI number prominently on containers, stickers, or carry bags.

Food-Grade Packaging Materials

All packaging materials in direct contact with food must be food-grade and compliant with Indian regulations. This covers containers, lids, inner wraps, foils, and any paper or film that touches the food directly. Ola's merchant guidelines reference BIS standards for food-contact materials and require that packaging does not alter food taste, colour, or safety.

Tamper-Evidence Requirements

Ola has adopted tamper-evident packaging as a standard requirement for its food delivery partners. Acceptable methods include adhesive seal stickers, heat-sealed containers, stapled or sealed carry bags, and branded tamper-evident tape. Ola's customer feedback system specifically asks about tamper evidence, and restaurants that consistently fail to seal orders receive compliance notifications.

Spillage Prevention

Ola's quality metrics track spillage complaints per restaurant. The platform expects all liquid and semi-liquid items to be packed in genuinely leak-proof containers. Restaurants with above-average spillage rates face quality warnings and potential visibility reduction in the app's restaurant listings.

Packaging Considerations Unique to Ola

Shared Delivery Network

Because Ola uses its broader logistics network for food delivery, your food order might be handled by a delivery partner whose primary experience is in ride-hailing rather than food delivery. This partner may not instinctively know to keep food containers upright or to avoid placing heavy items on top of food packages. Your packaging needs to compensate for this by being structurally sound and resistant to orientation changes.

Vehicle Variety

Ola delivery partners may use two-wheelers, three-wheelers, or even four-wheelers depending on the order size and location. Each vehicle type creates different handling conditions. A food package on a motorcycle experiences more tilting and vibration than one in a car. Package for the worst-case scenario: design your packaging to survive a motorcycle delivery even if the order might be delivered by car.

Delivery Speed Focus

Ola's operational efficiency focus means the platform pushes for fast pickups. Your packaging needs to be efficient to assemble. Pre-staged containers, standardised packing workflows, and quick-seal tamper evidence are essential for meeting Ola's pickup time expectations without compromising packaging quality.

Leak-Proofing for Gravies

Indian food is gravy-heavy, and gravy is the primary source of delivery packaging complaints across all platforms including Ola. For gravy dishes, use the double-seal method: stretch cling film across the container opening, then press the lid over the cling film. This creates two barriers against leakage. For extra security on long-distance deliveries, wrap the entire sealed container in aluminium foil, which also helps retain heat.

Carry Bag Selection

Use non-woven or heavy-duty carry bags for Ola orders. The bag should comfortably hold all containers for a typical order with room for the delivery partner to grip the handles without squeezing the contents. D-cut non-woven bags in 12x16 or 14x18 inch sizes work well for most orders. For larger orders or heavy items, use bags with reinforced handles.

Packaging and Customer Experience on Ola

First Impression Matters

When the customer receives their Ola delivery, the carry bag is the first thing they see. A clean, sturdy bag with a visible brand sticker creates a positive first impression. Inside, neatly arranged and clearly labelled containers continue the positive experience. This matters because Ola's rating system captures the customer's overall satisfaction, and packaging is a significant component of that impression.

Complete Orders Reduce Complaints

Missing items are a common source of complaints on food delivery platforms. For Ola orders, include a packing checklist inside the carry bag. This simple addition serves two purposes: your packing staff uses it as a verification tool, and the customer can quickly confirm all items are present without having to contact support.

Accompaniments and Extras

Always include the appropriate cutlery, napkins, and condiments with every order. For specific dishes, include the expected accompaniments: raita with biryani in a small sauce container, chutney with South Indian items, and sauces with Chinese food. Missing accompaniments generate disproportionate dissatisfaction relative to their cost.

Operational Workflow for Ola Orders

Efficient packing workflows are essential during peak hours when Ola, Swiggy, and Zomato orders arrive simultaneously.

Unified Packing Station

Set up a single packing station that handles all platform orders with the same container set and workflow. The only difference between platforms should be the order ticket source. Containers, seals, bags, and accessories should be pre-staged at the packing station regardless of which platform the order comes from.

Packing Sequence

  1. Read order ticket and identify all items
  2. Select appropriate containers for each item
  3. Fill containers to 80-85% capacity
  4. Apply cling film where needed (gravies, liquids)
  5. Press lids firmly and verify seal
  6. Apply tamper-evident stickers
  7. Label containers with dish names
  8. Place in carry bag with hot and cold items separated
  9. Add cutlery, napkins, and accompaniments
  10. Seal carry bag and place at handoff point

This ten-step sequence, when practised consistently, processes an order in 2-4 minutes and produces consistent results across all delivery platforms.

Handling Quality Issues and Complaints

Monitor your Ola merchant dashboard for packaging-related feedback. When complaints appear, categorise them by type: spillage, temperature, missing items, tamper evidence, or general quality. Track the frequency and timing of each category. Patterns that emerge from this tracking will point you toward specific process improvements rather than general packaging overhauls.

Common fixes include switching to higher-quality containers for frequently leaked items, adding an extra sealing step for gravy dishes during peak hours when rush can lead to shortcuts, and implementing a two-person check system for combo orders where missing items are common.

Restaurants that treat packaging as a continuous improvement process rather than a set-and-forget decision consistently outperform competitors on all delivery platforms, including Ola.
