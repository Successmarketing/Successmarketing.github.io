Pizza Box Dimensions: Complete Size Guide in Inches and cm

June 10, 2025 12 min read How-To

Getting the pizza box size wrong is one of the most visible packaging mistakes a pizzeria can make. A box that is too large lets the pizza slide around during delivery, damaging toppings and presentation. A box that is too tight squeezes the crust and makes it difficult to remove slices. And a box that is too shallow crushes toppings or smudges cheese against the lid.

This guide covers every standard pizza box dimension available in the Indian market, with measurements in both inches and centimetres, along with recommendations for box height, material thickness, and which size suits which type of pizza.

How Pizza Box Sizes Are Measured

Pizza box sizes are described by the internal length of one side of the box, measured in inches. Because pizza boxes are square, this single measurement defines the box. A "10-inch pizza box" has internal dimensions of approximately 10 x 10 inches. The external dimensions are slightly larger due to the thickness of the corrugated board.

The box height (depth) is a separate measurement and is critical for pizza delivery. Too shallow and the box presses against the toppings. Too deep and the pizza loses heat faster because of the extra air space. The sweet spot for most pizzas is 40-50 mm (roughly 1.5-2 inches) of internal height.

Pizza Sizes in the Indian Market

Box Height: The Overlooked Dimension

Many pizzeria owners obsess over box length while neglecting height, which is equally important. Here is a practical guide to box height selection:

Pizza Type Recommended Box Height Reasoning
Thin crust 35-40 mm Low profile; minimal clearance needed above toppings
Regular / hand tossed 42-48 mm Standard height for most pizza styles
Deep dish / pan pizza 50-55 mm Thick base and loaded toppings need more clearance
Stuffed crust 48-52 mm Stuffed edges add height at the crust
Pizza with sides in box 55-65 mm Extra depth to accommodate garlic bread, dip cups, etc.

Corrugated Board Specifications

Pizza boxes are made from corrugated cardboard, and the quality of the board directly affects the box's ability to protect the pizza during delivery. There are three key specifications to consider:

Flute type refers to the wave pattern of the corrugated medium between the flat liners. For pizza boxes, B-flute (2.5-3 mm thickness) and E-flute (1.5-2 mm thickness) are the most common. B-flute provides better cushioning and insulation but takes up more space. E-flute offers a smoother surface for printing and is adequate for shorter delivery distances.

Board grade is measured in GSM or by burst factor. For pizza boxes, a minimum of 150 GSM kraft liner with 120 GSM fluting is recommended. Heavier grades (180 GSM liner, 150 GSM fluting) are better for larger boxes (12 inches and above) and for long-distance delivery.

Coating is essential because pizza releases moisture and grease. Most pizza boxes have a grease-resistant coating on the inside surface. Special Pizza Box Formats

Half-and-Half Boxes

Some pizzerias, particularly those with two-flavour options, use boxes with a centre divider that keeps the two halves separate. These are typically available in 10-inch and 12-inch sizes and are a premium option that costs 20-30% more than a standard box.

Slice Boxes

The growing trend of pizza-by-the-slice requires triangular or trapezoidal boxes sized for individual slices. These are typically 15-20 cm along the outer curve and 20-25 cm along the length. They are increasingly popular with street food vendors and kiosk operations.

Mini Pizza Boxes

With the rise of personal-size pizzas and pizza-based snacks, 5-inch and 6-inch mini boxes are becoming more common. These are used for small flatbread pizzas, naan pizzas, and other fusion items that have become popular with food trucks and cafes.

Sizing for Multiple Pizzas

Party orders and catering often require stacking multiple pizza boxes. When stacking, ensure that box sizes are compatible. The industry best practice is to stack from largest at the bottom to smallest at the top. Most delivery bags are designed for standard stacking of 3-4 boxes of the same size.

Some pizzerias use a larger outer box (16-inch) to hold two smaller pizzas (7-8 inch each) side by side for couple's or combo deals. This requires careful measurement to ensure the pizzas fit without shifting.

Printing and Branding on Pizza Boxes

Pizza boxes are among the most visible pieces of food packaging because they often sit on a dining table for extended periods during a group meal. This makes them exceptional branding real estate. The flat surface of a pizza box lid is ideal for logo placement, menu highlights, QR codes linking to your ordering app, and social media handles.
