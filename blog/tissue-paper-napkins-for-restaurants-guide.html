Tissue Paper Napkins for Restaurants: The Complete Buying and Usage Guide

March 5, 2025 10 min read Tissue & Napkins

Walk into any restaurant in India and you will find tissue napkins on every table. They are one of the most basic yet essential supplies in the food service industry. Despite their everyday presence, most restaurant owners spend very little time thinking about which napkins to buy, how much to stock, or whether they are overspending. This guide covers everything you need to know about tissue paper napkins -- from understanding GSM ratings and ply counts to calculating the right quantity for your business and finding the best wholesale deals.

Why Tissue Napkins Deserve Your Attention

Tissue napkins are the most frequently touched item on a restaurant table after the food itself. They directly affect the customer experience in several ways:

  • Hygiene perception: A good-quality, clean white napkin signals that the restaurant cares about cleanliness. A thin, rough napkin does the opposite.
  • Practical function: Indian food -- especially street food, tandoori items, and oily curries -- requires effective napkins. A napkin that tears on first wipe or cannot absorb oil is functionally useless.
  • Branding opportunity: Printed napkins with your restaurant's name and logo serve as low-cost brand reinforcement.
  • Cost management: Napkins that are too thin get used 2-3 per customer instead of 1. The cost per customer ends up the same or higher than buying better napkins in the first place.

Understanding Tissue Paper Specifications

GSM (Grams per Square Meter)

GSM is the single most important specification for tissue paper. It indicates the weight and, by extension, the thickness and quality of the paper. Here is what different GSM ranges mean:

GSM Range Quality Level Best Use Feel
13-17 GSM Economy Street food stalls, budget eateries Thin, rough
17-20 GSM Standard Most restaurants, cafes, cloud kitchens Adequate softness
20-25 GSM Premium Fine dining, hotels, upscale cafes Soft, absorbent
25+ GSM Luxury 5-star hotels, high-end events Very soft, cloth-like

Ply Count

Ply refers to the number of layers bonded together in a single napkin:

  • 1-ply: Single layer. Most economical. Suitable for basic wiping in fast-food joints and street stalls.
  • 2-ply: The standard for most Indian restaurants. Two layers bonded together offer a good balance of absorbency, softness, and cost.
  • 3-ply: Premium option. Three layers provide superior softness and absorbency. Used in upscale establishments.

A 2-ply napkin at 17-20 GSM is the sweet spot for the vast majority of restaurants in India. It costs only marginally more than 1-ply but performs significantly better, reducing per-customer usage.

Types of Tissue Napkins for Restaurants

1. Plain White Napkins

The workhorse of the industry. Plain white napkins are the most affordable and universally acceptable. They work in every setting from a dhaba to a fine dining restaurant. For dine-in restaurants, they are placed in tabletop dispensers or napkin holders.

2. Printed / Branded Napkins

Custom-printed napkins with your restaurant logo, name, or tagline serve double duty -- functional use and brand marketing. Every time a customer reaches for a napkin, they see your brand. The cost premium for printing is typically 15-30% above plain napkins when ordered in bulk (minimum 10,000-50,000 pieces per design).

3. Colored Napkins

Available in various colors to match restaurant decor or event themes. Popular for wedding catering, birthday parties, and themed events. Slightly more expensive than white, and the dye used must be food-safe certified.

4. Interfold / Pop-up Napkins

Designed for napkin dispensers, these napkins are interfolded so that pulling one napkin automatically presents the next. This controlled dispensing reduces waste by 20-30% compared to loose napkin holders where customers tend to grab multiple napkins at once.

5. Wet Wipes / Moist Towelettes

Pre-moistened towelettes in individual sachets. Used in higher-end restaurants, airlines, and catering services. They provide a superior cleaning experience but cost 5-10 times more than dry napkins. Many Indian restaurants offer them alongside regular napkins for post-meal hand cleaning.

Calculating Your Restaurant's Napkin Requirement

The right quantity depends on your restaurant type and customer volume. Here is a practical framework:

Dine-In Restaurants

Formula: Customers per day x 3 napkins per customer x 30 days x 1.2 (buffer)

A restaurant serving 200 dine-in customers daily: 200 x 3 x 30 x 1.2 = 21,600 napkins per month

Delivery / Takeaway Kitchens

Formula: Orders per day x 2 napkins per order x 30 days x 1.1 (buffer)

A cloud kitchen processing 150 delivery orders daily: 150 x 2 x 30 x 1.1 = 9,900 napkins per month

Catering Services

Formula: Guests per event x 4 napkins per guest (for multi-course meals)

A wedding catering for 500 guests: 500 x 4 = 2,000 napkins per event

Reducing Napkin Waste

Install interfold napkin dispensers instead of open napkin holders. Studies show dispensers reduce usage by 25-30% because customers take one napkin at a time rather than grabbing a fistful. The dispenser cost pays for itself within 2-3 months through reduced napkin consumption.

Choosing the Right Napkin for Your Business

Here is a practical decision guide based on business type:

  • Street food / chaat stalls: 1-ply, 13-15 GSM, smallest size. Keep costs minimal -- your customers are eating quickly and on the go.
  • Casual restaurants / dhabas: 1-ply or 2-ply, 15-17 GSM. Functional is the priority. Use dispensers to control usage.
  • Mid-range restaurants: 2-ply, 17-20 GSM, 27x27 or 30x30 cm. The standard choice that balances quality and cost.
  • Cloud kitchens / delivery: 2-ply, 17 GSM, 27x27 cm. Include 2 napkins per delivery order in a sealed cutlery kit with a disposable spoon.
  • Fine dining / hotels: 2-ply or 3-ply, 20+ GSM, 33x33 cm or larger. Consider branded napkins.
  • Event catering: 2-ply minimum, size and color matched to the event theme. Order well in advance for custom prints.

Storage and Shelf Life

Tissue napkins have a long shelf life if stored correctly:

  • Store in a dry, well-ventilated area. Moisture is the enemy -- damp napkins develop mold and become unusable.
  • Keep away from strong odors. Tissue paper absorbs smells easily. Store separately from cleaning chemicals, spices, and cooking areas.
  • Maintain original packaging until use to prevent dust accumulation.
  • Rotate stock using FIFO (first in, first out) to prevent yellowing of older stock.
  • Ideal storage temperature: 15-25 degrees Celsius with relative humidity below 65%.
