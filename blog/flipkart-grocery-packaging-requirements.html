Flipkart Grocery Packaging Requirements for Food Sellers in India

September 8, 2025 14 min read Industry

Flipkart has aggressively expanded into the grocery and food products space through Flipkart Supermart, Flipkart Quick, and its general marketplace. For food brands and sellers in India, Flipkart represents a significant sales channel, second only to Amazon in e-commerce reach. But selling food products on Flipkart comes with packaging requirements that are distinct from both restaurant delivery platforms and Amazon's standards.

This guide walks through Flipkart's food packaging requirements, the practical considerations for getting your products warehouse-ready, and the packaging strategies that help your products survive the Flipkart supply chain while looking great when they reach the customer.

Flipkart's Food Product Packaging Standards

Regulatory Compliance: FSSAI and Legal Metrology

Flipkart requires all food sellers to have a valid FSSAI license. The FSSAI license number, along with the FSSAI logo, must be printed on the product packaging. Beyond FSSAI, Flipkart also requires compliance with the Legal Metrology Act, which governs how products are measured, labelled, and sold in India. For food products, this means:

  • Net quantity must be declared in metric units (grams, kilograms, millilitres, litres)
  • MRP (Maximum Retail Price) including all taxes must be printed on the package
  • The package must identify the manufacturer or packer with full address
  • Month and year of manufacture or packaging must be declared
  • Best before or use by date must be clearly printed
  • Customer care details (phone number or email) must be provided

Products that fail to meet Legal Metrology requirements will be rejected during Flipkart's quality checks, regardless of how well they meet other packaging criteria.

Flipkart Supermart Specific Requirements

Flipkart Supermart operates its own fulfilment model for grocery items. Products supplied to Supermart go through Flipkart's grocery warehouses and are delivered through Flipkart's own delivery network. The packaging requirements for Supermart are more stringent than general marketplace listings:

  • Minimum shelf life: Products must have at least 60% of their total shelf life remaining at the time of delivery to Flipkart's warehouse. For a product with 12 months shelf life, it must reach the warehouse within 4.8 months of manufacture.
  • Outer packaging integrity: Products must arrive at the warehouse in perfect condition. Dented, torn, or damaged outer packaging leads to rejection. Use corrugated cases for shipping that protect the primary product packaging.
  • Case packing: Products shipped to Flipkart warehouses must be in standardised cases (typically corrugated boxes) with consistent unit counts. The case must be labelled with product details, quantity, batch number, and expiry date.
  • Barcode requirements: Products must carry a scannable EAN barcode. The barcode must be placed where it can be scanned without opening the case packaging.

Flipkart Quick Requirements

Flipkart Quick promises delivery within minutes for select grocery items. Products in the Quick catalogue are stored in hyperlocal dark stores. Packaging for Quick products needs to be compact (dark stores have limited space), easily identifiable by dark store staff for fast picking, and robust enough to survive rapid handling during the picking and packing process.

Secondary and Transit Packaging

The journey from your facility to the customer's doorstep on Flipkart involves multiple handling points: your warehouse to Flipkart's hub, hub to sorting centre, sorting centre to delivery hub, and delivery hub to the customer. At each point, your product is handled, stacked, and potentially compressed by surrounding packages. Transit packaging must account for this multi-stage journey.

  • Corrugated cases: Use 3-ply corrugated boxes for light products and 5-ply for heavy or fragile products. The case must hold products snugly to prevent shifting during transit.
  • Inner cushioning: Glass jars and fragile items need bubble wrap, foam inserts, or corrugated dividers inside the shipping case.
  • Void fill: Empty space inside shipping cases allows products to move and collide. Fill voids with crumpled paper, air pillows, or foam peanuts.
  • Tape and sealing: Use strong packing tape (minimum 48mm width) on all case openings and seams. Cross-tape the bottom for heavy products.

Common Flipkart Packaging Rejections and How to Avoid Them

Rejection: Label Information Mismatch

Flipkart's catalogue team compares your product listing details with the physical product label. Any mismatch in product name, weight, ingredients, or nutritional information leads to listing rejection. Before creating your Flipkart listing, photograph your final label and use it as the reference for all listing details. Keep a master document that tracks the exact text on your label.

Rejection: Insufficient Shelf Life

Products arriving at Flipkart warehouses with less than the required remaining shelf life are rejected and returned at the seller's cost. Maintain tight production-to-dispatch cycles. Do not stockpile finished goods for weeks before sending to Flipkart. Ship fresh batches regularly rather than large, aging batches infrequently.

Rejection: Damaged Products on Arrival

Products that arrive at Flipkart's warehouse with damaged packaging are rejected. This includes dented cans, crushed boxes, torn pouches, and loose lids. Invest in proper transit packaging. The cost of a good corrugated case and inner cushioning is far less than the cost of returned shipments and lost sales.

Rejection: Missing or Unscannable Barcodes

If Flipkart's warehouse staff cannot scan the product barcode, the product is flagged for manual processing or rejected. Place barcodes on flat surfaces, avoid placing them on curved areas of containers, and ensure sufficient print contrast (black bars on white background). Test barcode scannability before shipping to Flipkart.

Branding and Packaging Design for Flipkart

Stand Out in Search Results

On Flipkart's grocery listings, your product competes visually with dozens of alternatives. Your packaging design, as shown in product images, is the primary differentiator. Invest in professional packaging design that is clean, informative, and visually distinctive. Avoid cluttered designs that become illegible in thumbnail images. Use bold colours and clear typography for your brand name and product name.

Photography-Optimised Packaging

Flipkart product images follow specific guidelines: white background, product filling at least 85% of the image frame, no props or lifestyle elements in the main image. Design your packaging so it looks good under these constraints. Front-facing packaging that shows the brand name, product name, and key benefit clearly translates well to e-commerce product photography.

Consistency Across SKUs

If you sell multiple products on Flipkart, maintain packaging design consistency across your range. Consistent colours, logo placement, and design language help customers recognise your brand across different product listings. This builds brand recall and encourages multi-product purchases.

Cost Management for Flipkart Food Packaging

Packaging costs for e-commerce food products typically run higher than for traditional retail because of the additional transit packaging layer. Here is how to manage costs:

Optimise Packaging Size

Flipkart charges sellers based on volumetric weight, which considers package dimensions as well as actual weight. An oversized box for a small product increases your shipping costs unnecessarily. Design your packaging to be as compact as possible while still protecting the product.

Standardise Across Channels

If you sell on both Flipkart and Amazon, use the same primary packaging for both platforms. Customise only the secondary packaging and shipping labels as needed. This consolidates your packaging procurement and reduces per-unit costs.

Wholesale Packaging Procurement

Buy containers, pouches, and packaging materials in bulk from a wholesale supplier. Volume discounts of 15-25% are standard for bulk orders. For a food seller shipping 500-1000 units per month to Flipkart, this saving directly improves your per-unit profitability.

Design for Reusability

Packaging that customers can reuse (resealable pouches, storage containers, attractive boxes) generates goodwill and reduces the perception of waste. This can translate to better reviews and repeat purchases, improving your long-term return on packaging investment.

Checklist: Getting Your Food Product Flipkart-Ready

  1. Verify FSSAI license is current and covers your product categories
  2. Confirm Legal Metrology compliance on all packaging labels
  3. Print FSSAI logo, license number, and all mandatory information on primary packaging
  4. Ensure MRP, manufacturing date, and expiry date are clearly printed
  5. Place scannable EAN barcode on a flat, visible surface
  6. Select primary packaging with appropriate barrier properties for your food type
  7. Design transit packaging that survives Flipkart's multi-stage handling
  8. Test packaging with a drop test (1 metre onto concrete)
  9. Verify that physical label matches your Flipkart product listing exactly
  10. Calculate remaining shelf life at time of warehouse delivery and ensure compliance

Getting packaging right from the start prevents the costly cycle of rejections, returns, and relisting that plagues unprepared food sellers on Flipkart.
