Kraft Paper Packaging for Food Business: Complete Guide for 2025

May 18, 2025 15 min read Eco-Friendly

Walk into any modern food court, artisan bakery, or upscale cloud kitchen, and you will notice a common thread: brown kraft paper packaging has become the visual language of quality food service. This is not accidental. Kraft paper -- named from the German word for "strength" -- offers a rare combination of structural performance, printability, food safety, environmental credentials, and brand appeal that few other materials can match at comparable cost.

For Indian food businesses navigating the post-plastic-ban landscape, kraft paper packaging represents the most accessible and versatile step toward sustainable operations. This guide covers the material science, product categories, safety standards, cost structures, and sourcing strategies that procurement managers need.

What Is Kraft Paper and Why Is It Different?

Kraft paper is produced through the kraft pulping process (also called sulphate process), which treats wood chips with a mixture of sodium hydroxide and sodium sulphide to break down lignin and separate cellulose fibres. The resulting pulp retains longer, stronger fibres than other paper-making processes, which gives kraft paper its signature high tensile strength and tear resistance.

The natural brown colour of kraft paper comes from the residual lignin left in the pulp. Unbleached kraft paper (the standard "brown bag" colour) is stronger and more environmentally sound than bleached (white) kraft because the bleaching process weakens fibres and introduces chemical residues. For food packaging, unbleached kraft with a food-grade inner surface is the default specification.

Types of Kraft Paper Used in Food Packaging

Natural Brown Kraft

The workhorse of food packaging. Unbleached, high-strength kraft paper in the 60-120 GSM range is used for bags, wraps, tray liners, and general food wrapping. Its natural brown appearance has become synonymous with artisanal and eco-conscious branding. For food contact, it must be sourced from virgin pulp (not recycled) to ensure compliance with FSSAI standards.

MG (Machine Glazed) Kraft

One side of this paper is polished to a smooth, glossy finish during manufacturing. This creates a surface that resists grease penetration better than uncoated kraft, making it suitable for wrapping oily Indian snacks like samosas, kachoris, and pakoras. The smooth surface also prints more crisply, which benefits branding.

PE-Coated Kraft

A thin layer of polyethylene is laminated onto one or both sides of kraft paper to provide waterproofing and grease resistance. This is the standard material for paper cups, soup containers, and any application where liquid contact is sustained. The PE coating adds approximately 15-20% to the base kraft cost. A downside is that PE coating complicates recycling and composting, though newer facilities can handle separation.

PLA-Coated Kraft

An emerging alternative where the PE coating is replaced with Polylactic Acid (PLA), a plant-based bioplastic. PLA-coated kraft is industrially compostable, making it the most environmentally complete option. Currently, it carries a 25-40% premium over PE-coated kraft, and availability in India is growing but not yet universal.

Wax-Coated Kraft

Traditional wax coating (food-grade paraffin or soy wax) provides moderate moisture and grease resistance without plastic. Wax-coated kraft is used for sandwich wraps, burger wraps, and food basket liners. It is not suitable for hot liquid applications. For more details, see our wax-coated paper packaging guide.

Kraft Board / Kraft Card

Thicker kraft material (250-450 GSM) formed into rigid sheets for die-cutting into boxes, trays, sleeves, and folding cartons. Kraft board is the material behind most takeaway meal boxes, cake boxes, pizza slice holders, and branded packaging. It offers excellent structural integrity and prints beautifully.

Food Safety Compliance for Kraft Paper

Paper and paperboard food contact materials in India must comply with FSSAI Regulation 2.4.1, which references IS 6615 (Acid-Soluble Method for Determination of Lead and Cadmium) and IS 6608 for food-grade paper. Key requirements include:

  • Virgin fibre requirement: Food-contact kraft must be made from virgin wood pulp, not recycled paper. Recycled paperboard may contain printing inks, adhesives, and contaminants that migrate into food.
  • Heavy metal limits: Lead must not exceed 1 mg/kg, cadmium must not exceed 0.5 mg/kg, and total heavy metals must not exceed 100 mg/kg of the paper material.
  • Fluorochemical limits: PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances) in grease-resistant coatings are facing global scrutiny. While India has not yet set specific PFAS limits for food paper, major brands are voluntarily moving to PFAS-free alternatives.
  • Printing ink safety: Inks used on the food-contact side must comply with IS 15495. Only food-grade inks (typically soy-based or water-based) are permitted. Printing on the outer surface has no food contact restrictions.

Custom Printing on Kraft Paper

One of kraft paper's standout advantages is its printability. The natural brown surface provides a built-in rustic aesthetic that requires minimal design effort to look premium. Common printing approaches for food businesses include:

Flexographic printing is the most cost-effective option for high-volume runs (typically 10,000+ units). It handles 1-4 colour designs well and is the standard for printed paper cups, bags, and wrapping sheets. Setup costs range from Rs 5,000-15,000 per design, but per-unit costs are minimal at volume.

Offset printing produces higher quality output with finer detail and broader colour range. It is preferred for premium packaging like cake boxes and branded meal boxes. Offset is economical at runs of 2,000-5,000+ units.

Digital printing is the best option for short runs (50-500 units) and personalised packaging. While per-unit cost is higher, there are no plate or setup charges. This makes it ideal for testing designs, seasonal promotions, or event-specific branding.

Stamp/sticker branding is the lowest-cost entry point. Businesses purchase plain kraft packaging and apply branded stickers or rubber stamps. This works well for startups and small operations that need flexibility without large minimum order quantities.

Sourcing and Cost Optimisation

For Indian food businesses, kraft paper packaging costs can be optimised through several strategies:

  • Buy in bulk from wholesale distributors. Kraft packaging prices drop 15-25% when ordering in cases or cartons versus individual packs. A wholesale partner like Success Marketing, which stocks the full range of kraft products, can offer consolidated pricing across your entire packaging needs.
  • Standardise sizes. Using 2-3 standard container sizes across your menu reduces SKU complexity and improves volume pricing. A 750ml kraft box can work for biryani, noodles, and rice bowls.
  • Order printed packaging in advance. Printing costs are lowest when you can plan 4-6 weeks ahead and order in larger batches.
  • Use unbranded for low-visibility items. Wrapping sheets, tray liners, and napkins rarely need custom printing. Use plain kraft for these and reserve printing budgets for customer-facing boxes and bags.

Limitations and How to Mitigate Them

Kraft paper is not a universal solution. Its limitations include susceptibility to moisture (without coating), limited heat tolerance for liquid-heavy foods, and structural weakness when wet. Mitigate these by selecting the right variant for each application: PE-coated for liquids, wax-coated for moderate grease exposure, and plain kraft only for dry foods and short-contact wrapping.

Storage also requires attention. Kraft packaging absorbs ambient moisture in humid environments, causing softening and reduced structural performance. Store in sealed poly-lined boxes in a dry, ventilated area. Maintain first-in-first-out rotation with a maximum stock holding of 3-4 months.

For applications where kraft paper falls short -- such as heavy-duty catering or extended-hold hot food -- consider pairing it with corrugated cardboard solutions or bagasse containers for maximum versatility.
